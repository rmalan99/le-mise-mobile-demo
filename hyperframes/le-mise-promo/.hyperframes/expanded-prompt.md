# Le Mise Premium Promo Production Breakdown

## Title + Style Block

Le Mise becomes a warm, editorial product-commercial for a modern food and dining mobile app. The video uses the established brand palette: coral `#ff6b6b`, fresh green `#2dbe7f`, golden accent `#ffc83d`, aqua `#00b8c9`, info blue `#0ea5e9`, warm cream `#fff6eb`, soft mint `#f7fdf9`, dark slate `#1f2937`, and muted slate `#4b5563`. The tone is elegant, refined, aspirational, and calm, with a realistic iPhone 12 Pro mockup as the hero throughout.

Typography uses Fraunces for the editorial product voice and Nunito for approachable app UI and captions, echoing the app's existing rounded warmth while lifting the commercial into a more premium register.

## Rhythm Declaration

Rhythm pattern: slow-build-BUILD-PEAK-breathe-resolve-CTA across 28 seconds. The motion language is calm and premium: soft blur crossfades, gentle zooms, lateral parallax, layered phone-screen reveals, and restrained focus-pull transitions.

## Global Rules

- Every scene keeps the phone realistic and central, using iPhone 12 Pro proportions with a notch, side buttons, camera details, metallic rim, glass screen, and soft floor shadow.
- Every scene has 3 depth layers: warm gradient and editorial decoratives in the background, the phone and product UI in the midground, and premium callouts or labels in the foreground.
- Transitions are CSS blur crossfades and focus pulls, 0.65-0.8s, using `sine.inOut`, `power1.inOut`, and `power2.out`.
- Motion is subtle: no aggressive movement, no flashy effects, no neon, no clutter.
- Food imagery is vector/illustrative and locally rendered with CSS/SVG-like shapes for reliability.

## Scene Beats

### 1. Hero Intro

Concept: The phone appears as a curated culinary object on a cream editorial backdrop, like a premium product launch hero shot. The viewer should immediately understand Le Mise as calm, delicious, and polished.

Mood direction: Modern culinary lifestyle campaign, warm studio light, high-end app-store promo.

Depth layers: BG radial cream/coral/mint glows, ghost brand word, fine registration rules; MG centered iPhone; FG Le Mise logo lockup and small feature chips.

Animation choreography: Phone floats up and settles, logo fades and tightens tracking, feature chips drift into place, glows breathe slowly.

Transition out: Blur crossfade into app-opening moment.

### 2. App Opening Moment

Concept: The app wakes up with a refined first impression: a personalized greeting, curated hero card, and a quiet signal that cooking can feel confident and effortless.

Mood direction: Boutique concierge, morning kitchen light, minimal but alive.

Depth layers: BG warm cream panels and soft mint aura; MG phone home screen; FG callout for curated daily inspiration.

Animation choreography: Header draws in, hero card rises, recommendation pills cascade softly, notification dot glows once.

Transition out: Focus-pull blur into browsing flow.

### 3. Browsing Flow

Concept: The viewer glides through curated categories, featured dishes, and restaurant-style cards, seeing Le Mise as a tasteful discovery surface.

Mood direction: Editorial menu spread with tactile cards.

Depth layers: BG category labels and warm rule lines; MG phone with stacked recipe cards; FG external card fragments drifting beside the phone.

Animation choreography: Category chips slide horizontally, dish cards cascade upward, floating cards parallax gently.

Transition out: Soft coral light leak into dish exploration.

### 4. Dish Exploration

Concept: A premium dish detail page makes food feel desirable and trustworthy, showing imagery, ratings, ingredients, price, and action.

Mood direction: Restaurant menu close-up, detail-rich but uncluttered.

Depth layers: BG oversized ingredient words, golden accent rings; MG phone detail screen; FG rating and ingredient callouts.

Animation choreography: Dish image blooms, detail sheet slides into view, ingredient tags populate one by one, price and action button settle last.

Transition out: Focus-pull to personalization.

### 5. Favorites + Personalization

Concept: Saved dishes and tailored recommendations create a personal culinary library rather than a generic recipe list.

Mood direction: Personal taste profile, curated favorites shelf.

Depth layers: BG heart outlines and soft mint glow; MG phone favorites screen; FG recommendation strips and preference tags.

Animation choreography: Saved hearts fill, recommendation strips glide in, taste tags count up visually.

Transition out: Calm blur crossfade into checkout/reservation.

### 6. Seamless Confirmation

Concept: The commercial demonstrates trust: a clean reservation or checkout confirmation with no friction, clear timing, and reassurance.

Mood direction: Quiet hospitality, confirmation receipt, premium service.

Depth layers: BG pale blue and green trust marks; MG phone confirmation screen; FG security and timing callouts.

Animation choreography: Checklist draws in, confirmation card lifts, trusted badges fade in with a subtle scale, final checkmark resolves.

Transition out: Slow dissolve to closing brand shot.

### 7. Promotional Closing Shot

Concept: Le Mise ends as the hero brand: centered phone, elegant feature callouts, and a confident CTA that frames the app as daily dining inspiration.

Mood direction: App Store feature spot, premium closing card.

Depth layers: BG warm gradient, large ghost wordmark, final coral and green glows; MG centered phone; FG three feature callouts and CTA.

Animation choreography: Phone settles, feature callouts reveal around it, CTA breathes once, final frame holds confidently.

Transition out: Final fade to warm cream.

## Recurring Motifs

- Coral appears as the primary action and warmth cue.
- Fresh green signals health, freshness, and trust.
- Golden accent highlights premium recommendations and ratings.
- Aqua and info blue are used sparingly for reservation trust and system clarity.
- Thin editorial rules, rounded cards, and soft shadows repeat across scenes.

## Negative Prompt

No cyberpunk, no dark neon, no glitch, no aggressive kinetic type, no unrealistic phone proportions, no cluttered card grids, no generic stock-photo collage, no emoji icons, no jump cuts, no cheap geometric transition patterns.
